The rules

Hockey is a high-speed, team-based sport played on ice, where players aim to score goals by hitting a puck into the opposing team’s net using a stick. The game emphasizes skill, strategy, and physical play, and it’s governed by structured rules to keep play fair and safe. Each match consists of three periods, and players must follow specific guidelines regarding movement, scoring, and conduct.

  • Game Structure: 3 periods of 20 minutes each, with intermissions between periods.
  • Team Composition: 6 players per side on the ice at a time — 5 skaters and 1 goalie.
  • Scoring: A goal is scored when the puck fully crosses the goal line between the goalposts.
  • Offside: Players must not enter the offensive zone before the puck does.
  • Icing: Shooting the puck across both the center line and the opponent’s goal line without it being touched results in a stoppage.
  • Face-offs: Used to resume play after a stoppage; players line up and battle for puck control.
  • Penalties: Infractions like tripping, high-sticking, or roughing result in time in the penalty box (usually 2 minutes), leaving the team short-handed.
  • Power Plays: When one team has more players on the ice due to an opponent’s penalty.
